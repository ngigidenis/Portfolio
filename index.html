Projects

BIOREMEDIATION OF INDUSTRIAL WASTEWATER CONTAMINATED WITH HEAVY METALS

Investigate the efficacy of microbial cultures in removing heavy metal pollutants from industrial wastewater through bioremediation processes.

In the project focused on the bioremediation of industrial wastewater contaminated with heavy metals, several key components were systematically addressed. Microbial Strain Selection involved the identification and optimization of specific bacterial strains with the capability to absorb and accumulate metal ions. The subsequent phase, Process Optimization, delved into the investigation of crucial parameters such as pH, temperature, and nutrient concentrations to heighten the efficiency of the bioremediation process. Employing advanced analytical methods, particularly ICP-MS, fell under Analytical Techniques, enabling the precise quantification of heavy metal concentrations both before and after treatment. Lastly, Environmental Impact Assessment played a vital role in evaluating the overall environmental impact and sustainability of the bioremediation process, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of its ecological implications.
